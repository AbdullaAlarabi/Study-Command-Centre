## Question 2: Model answer and marking guide

**Question:** Define marketing research and describe the four steps in the marketing research process.

**Model answer**

Marketing research is the systematic design, collection, analysis, and reporting of data relevant to a specific marketing situation. First, the company defines the problem and research objectives. Second, it develops a research plan specifying information needs, sources, methods, sample, instruments, and cost. Third, it implements the plan and collects and analyzes the data. Fourth, it interprets and reports the findings so managers can make decisions.

**Marking points**

- Correct definition
- Define problem/objectives
- Develop plan
- Implement and collect/analyze
- Interpret/report
- Correct order and brief explanation

**Source:** MKT112 Chapter 4, research-process slides

## Question 3: Model answer and marking guide

**Question:** Describe the five stages of the buyer decision process.

**Model answer**

The buyer decision process begins with need recognition, when a stimulus creates awareness of a problem or need. During information search, the buyer obtains information from personal, commercial, public, or experiential sources. In evaluation of alternatives, the buyer compares brands. In purchase decision, the buyer chooses, although other people or unexpected situations may affect the final choice. In postpurchase behavior, satisfaction or dissatisfaction depends on the relationship between expectations and perceived performance.

**Marking points**

- Need recognition
- Information search
- Evaluation of alternatives
- Purchase decision
- Postpurchase behavior
- Satisfaction/expectation link
- Correct sequence

**Source:** MKT112 Chapter 5, buyer decision process
