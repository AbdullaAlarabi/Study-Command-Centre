## Question 2: Model answer and marking guide

**Question:** Discuss the cultural, social, personal, and psychological factors that influence consumer buyer behavior.

**Model answer**

Cultural factors include culture and subculture, which shape basic values and behavior. Social factors include groups, social networks, word of mouth, opinion leaders, family, roles, and status. Personal factors include age and life stage, occupation, economic situation, lifestyle, personality, and self-concept. Psychological factors include motivation, perception, learning, beliefs, and attitudes. Marketers cannot fully control these forces but must understand them when designing offers and communication.

**Marking points**

- Cultural factors
- Social factors
- Personal factors
- Psychological factors
- Relevant examples
- Clear explanation of marketing importance

**Source:** MKT112 Chapter 5, factors influencing consumer behavior

## Question 3: Model answer and marking guide

**Question:** Explain cost-based pricing and the main internal and external factors affecting pricing decisions.

**Model answer**

Cost-based pricing sets price from the costs of producing, distributing, and selling the product plus a fair return. Fixed costs do not vary with output, variable costs change with production, and total cost is their sum. Cost-plus pricing adds a standard markup, while break-even or target-return pricing identifies the price and volume needed to cover cost or reach a target return. Internally, price must fit strategy, objectives, positioning, and the marketing mix. Externally, the firm considers market structure, demand and elasticity, competitors, economic conditions, resellers, government action, and social concerns.

**Marking points**

- Cost-based definition
- Fixed cost
- Variable cost
- Total cost
- Cost-plus
- Break-even/target return
- Internal factors
- External factors

**Source:** MKT112 Chapter 10, cost and pricing-factor sections
