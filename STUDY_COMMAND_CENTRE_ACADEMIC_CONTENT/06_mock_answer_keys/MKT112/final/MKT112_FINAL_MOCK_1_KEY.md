## Question 2: Model answer and marking guide

**Question:** Define the promotion mix and explain its five major tools.

**Model answer**

The promotion mix is the specific blend of tools a company uses to engage consumers, communicate customer value, and build relationships. Advertising is paid nonpersonal presentation by an identified sponsor. Sales promotion provides short-term incentives. Personal selling is direct interaction by the sales force. Public relations builds good relations, favorable publicity, and corporate image and handles unfavorable events. Direct and digital marketing engages targeted customers immediately and interactively.

**Marking points**

- Promotion-mix definition
- Advertising
- Sales promotion
- Personal selling
- Public relations
- Direct and digital marketing
- Brief explanation of each

**Source:** MKT112 Chapter 14, promotion mix

## Question 3: Model answer and marking guide

**Question:** Discuss online, social media, and mobile marketing.

**Model answer**

Online marketing uses websites, online advertising, search ads, email, online video, and blogs. Marketing websites move customers toward an outcome, while branded community websites build engagement. Social media consists of online networks where users share content; it offers targeting, interaction, speed, engagement, and sharing but is user controlled and can backfire. Mobile marketing delivers messages and content through phones, tablets, and other devices and can reach customers anywhere and anytime. The channels should be coordinated rather than managed separately.

**Marking points**

- Online marketing definition and forms
- Website distinction
- Social media definition
- Social advantages
- Social limitations/user control
- Mobile marketing
- Need for integration

**Source:** MKT112 Chapter 17, digital channels

## Question 4: Model answer and marking guide

**Question:** Describe the steps in competitor analysis.

**Model answer**

Competitor analysis is the process of identifying, assessing, and selecting key competitors. First, the company identifies competitors using both product and broader market views. Second, it assesses competitor objectives, strategies, strengths and weaknesses, and reaction patterns. Strategic groups, benchmarking, customer value analysis, and competitive intelligence can support this assessment. Third, the company selects which competitors to attack or avoid, considering strong or weak, close or distant, and good or bad competitors.

**Marking points**

- Correct definition
- Identify competitors
- Product and market views
- Assess objectives
- Assess strategies
- Strengths/weaknesses
- Reaction patterns
- Select attack/avoid
- Supporting tools

**Source:** MKT112 Chapter 18, competitor analysis
