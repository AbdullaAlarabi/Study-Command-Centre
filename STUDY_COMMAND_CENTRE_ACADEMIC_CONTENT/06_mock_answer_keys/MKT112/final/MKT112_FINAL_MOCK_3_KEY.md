## Question 2: Model answer and marking guide

**Question:** Explain integrated marketing communications, the nine communication-process elements, and push versus pull strategy.

**Model answer**

Integrated marketing communications coordinates channels so the company delivers a clear, consistent, and compelling message. The nine communication elements are sender, encoding, message, media, decoding, receiver, response, feedback, and noise. A push strategy directs personal selling and trade promotion toward channel members so the product is moved toward consumers. A pull strategy directs advertising and consumer promotion toward final consumers so their demand pulls the product through the channel.

**Marking points**

- Definition and purpose of IMC
- Sender
- Encoding
- Message
- Media
- Decoding
- Receiver
- Response
- Feedback
- Noise
- Push
- Pull

**Source:** MKT112 Chapter 14, IMC and communication process

## Question 3: Model answer and marking guide

**Question:** Explain how marketers prepare a digital campaign and create an omni-channel strategy.

**Model answer**

Marketers begin by creating digital consumer personas that describe target customers’ needs, behavior, online habits, influences, goals, and concerns. They study consumer omni-channel navigation across the buying process and identify the digital and nondigital channels used at each stage. They use experimentation such as A/B testing to learn what works. An omni-channel strategy then integrates in-store, online, mobile, social, and other channels into a seamless experience. It requires alignment with the business model, structures, incentives, and marketing mix so channel teams support the total customer experience.

**Marking points**

- Persona
- Relevant persona details
- Customer journey/navigation
- Channel identification
- A/B testing
- Seamless integration
- Business-model alignment
- Structures/incentives
- Marketing-mix coordination

**Source:** MKT112 Chapter 17, campaign and omni-channel sections

## Question 4: Model answer and marking guide

**Question:** Compare competitor-centered, customer-centered, and market-centered companies, and explain the three broad competitive strategies.

**Model answer**

A competitor-centered company closely tracks rivals and responds to their moves, which can improve fighting ability but make the firm reactive. A customer-centered company focuses on customer needs and is better positioned to identify long-term opportunities. A market-centered company balances customer and competitor understanding and is the preferred orientation. The three broad competitive strategies are overall cost leadership, which seeks lower cost; differentiation, which offers unique value; and focus, which serves a narrow segment particularly well.

**Marking points**

- Competitor-centered definition and risk
- Customer-centered definition and strength
- Market-centered balance
- Overall cost leadership
- Differentiation
- Focus
- Clear comparison

**Source:** MKT112 Chapter 18, orientations and broad strategies
