## Question 2: Model answer and marking guide

**Question:** Describe the steps in developing effective marketing communications.

**Model answer**

The company first identifies the target audience, which may include buyers, users, decision makers, influencers, groups, or publics. It then determines communication objectives such as awareness, knowledge, liking, preference, conviction, or purchase. Next it designs the message, deciding what to say and how to present it. It chooses personal or nonpersonal channels and suitable media, selects a credible source, and collects feedback through awareness, recall, attitudes, purchasing, discussion, or visits.

**Marking points**

- Target audience
- Communication objectives
- Message design
- Channels/media
- Credible source
- Feedback
- Relevant objective hierarchy

**Source:** MKT112 Chapter 14, effective communication

## Question 3: Model answer and marking guide

**Question:** Define digital marketing and explain its benefits and main public-policy issues.

**Model answer**

Digital marketing uses the internet, mobile networks and devices, and social media to engage targeted consumers, communities, and businesses. Buyers gain convenience, privacy, information, choice, immediacy, interaction, and community. Sellers gain reach, speed, flexibility, personalization, lower cost, relationship building, and real-time response. Public-policy issues include irritation and spam, unfairness, deceptive content, fraud, identity theft, privacy, data integrity, and security. Trust requires transparency, customer choice, and responsible data practices.

**Marking points**

- Correct definition
- Buyer benefits
- Seller benefits
- Irritation
- Unfairness/deception
- Fraud
- Privacy
- Data integrity/security
- Transparency/choice

**Source:** MKT112 Chapter 17, benefits and policy sections

## Question 4: Model answer and marking guide

**Question:** Compare the strategies of market leaders, challengers, followers, and nichers.

**Model answer**

A market leader has the largest share and seeks to expand total demand, protect share, and expand share when profitable. A market challenger is a runner-up that attacks the leader or smaller firms directly or indirectly to gain share. A market follower seeks to hold share without provoking a major fight, often adapting successful ideas at lower cost while adding distinct value. A market nicher serves small profitable segments ignored by large firms and specializes by customer, product, service, quality, price, or marketing mix.

**Marking points**

- Leader definition and strategies
- Challenger definition and attack choice
- Follower definition and adaptation
- Nicher definition and specialization
- Clear comparison

**Source:** MKT112 Chapter 18, strategies by position
