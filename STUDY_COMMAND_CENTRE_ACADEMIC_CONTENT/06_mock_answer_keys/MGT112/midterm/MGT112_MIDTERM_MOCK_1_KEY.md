## Question 2: Model answer and marking guide

**Question:** Describe the six key elements of organizational structure.

**Model answer**

Organizational structure is the formal arrangement of jobs within an organization. Its six key elements are work specialization, which divides activities into separate tasks; departmentalization, which groups jobs; authority and responsibility, which define the right to direct work and the obligation to perform it; span of control, which is the number of employees a manager can supervise effectively; centralization versus decentralization, which determines where decisions are made; and formalization, which is the degree of job standardization and rule guidance.

**Marking points**

- Definition of organizational structure or design
- Work specialization
- Departmentalization
- Authority and responsibility
- Span of control
- Centralization/decentralization
- Formalization
- Brief explanation of each

**Source:** MGT112 Chapter 7, slides 4-15

## Question 3: Model answer and marking guide

**Question:** Describe the key components of the human resource management process and the important influences on that process.

**Model answer**

Human resource management is concerned with getting, training, motivating, and keeping competent employees. The process includes employment planning, recruitment, downsizing, selection, orientation, training and development, performance appraisal, and compensation and benefits. These activities help the organization obtain and retain high-performing employees. The process is influenced by external factors including law, economic conditions, technological change, and social expectations.

**Marking points**

- Correct HRM definition
- Employment planning
- Recruitment
- Downsizing
- Selection
- Orientation
- Training and development
- Performance appraisal
- Compensation and benefits
- External influences
