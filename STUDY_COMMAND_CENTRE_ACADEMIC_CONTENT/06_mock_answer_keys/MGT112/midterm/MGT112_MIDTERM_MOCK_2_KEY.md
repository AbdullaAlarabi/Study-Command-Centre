## Question 2: Model answer and marking guide

**Question:** Compare mechanistic and organic organizations and explain the conditions that favor each design.

**Model answer**

A mechanistic organization is rigid and controlled, with high specialization, fixed duties, many rules, formal communication, centralized decisions, narrow spans, and a tall hierarchy. An organic organization is adaptive and flexible, with collaboration, changing duties, few rules, informal communication, decentralized authority, wide spans, and a flatter structure. Mechanistic designs fit stable environments and cost-control strategies. Organic designs fit innovation, dynamic environments, and uncertainty. Size and technology also affect the appropriate structure.

**Marking points**

- Mechanistic definition and characteristics
- Organic definition and characteristics
- At least four clear contrasts
- Stable environment/cost control favors mechanistic
- Innovation/uncertainty favors organic
- Reference to size or technology

**Source:** MGT112 Chapter 7, slides 17-21

## Question 3: Model answer and marking guide

**Question:** Define a group and describe the five stages of group development.

**Model answer**

A group is two or more interacting and interdependent individuals who come together to achieve specific goals. In forming, members face uncertainty about purpose, structure, and leadership. In storming, conflict arises over control and direction. In norming, relationships, cohesion, identity, and shared expectations develop. In performing, the group structure functions and members focus on the task. In adjourning, a temporary group prepares to disband.

**Marking points**

- Correct group definition
- Forming
- Storming
- Norming
- Performing
- Adjourning
- Brief explanation of each stage

**Source:** MGT112 Chapter 9 and official MTA revision sheet
