## Question 2: Model answer and marking guide

**Question:** Define organizational behavior and explain its goals, main areas, and important employee outcomes.

**Model answer**

Organizational behavior is the study of the actions of people at work. It focuses on individual behavior, group behavior, and organizational systems such as structure, culture, and HR practices. Its goals are to explain, predict, and influence behavior. Important outcomes include productivity, absenteeism, turnover, organizational citizenship behavior, job satisfaction, and workplace misbehavior.

- Group behavior
- Organizational systems
- Explain

- Six outcomes

## Question 3: Model answer and marking guide

**Question:** Explain the job characteristics model and show how it can improve employee motivation.

**Model answer**

The model identifies five core dimensions: skill variety, task identity, task significance, autonomy, and feedback. Skill variety, task identity, and task significance create experienced meaningfulness. Autonomy creates responsibility for outcomes. Feedback provides knowledge of results. Jobs with strong dimensions can improve internal motivation, performance, satisfaction, and reduce absenteeism or turnover. Job enrichment vertically expands a job by adding planning and evaluation responsibilities.

**Marking points**

- Five dimensions
- Meaningfulness from first three
- Responsibility from autonomy
- Knowledge of results from feedback
- Motivational outcomes
- Job enrichment

**Source:** MGT112 Chapter 11, job characteristics section

## Question 4: Model answer and marking guide

**Question:** Explain situational leadership theory and path-goal theory.

**Model answer**

Situational leadership theory matches leadership style to follower readiness, meaning ability and willingness. Telling is high task and low relationship, selling is high task and high relationship, participating is low task and high relationship, and delegating is low task and low relationship. Path-goal theory says the leader helps followers reach goals and removes obstacles. The leader may be directive, supportive, participative, or achievement oriented depending on follower characteristics and the environment.

**Marking points**

- Follower readiness
- Telling
- Selling
- Participating
- Delegating
- Path-goal purpose
- Directive
- Supportive
- Participative
- Achievement oriented
- Contingency fit

**Source:** MGT112 Chapter 12, contingency theories
