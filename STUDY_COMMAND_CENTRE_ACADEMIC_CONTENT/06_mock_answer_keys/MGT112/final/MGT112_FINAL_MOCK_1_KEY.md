## Question 2: Model answer and marking guide

**Question:** Explain attribution theory and the main attribution errors.

**Model answer**

Attribution theory explains how people judge whether behavior is internally or externally caused. The judgment uses distinctiveness, consensus, and consistency. Distinctiveness asks whether the person behaves differently in other situations. Consensus asks whether other people behave similarly. Consistency asks whether the behavior repeats over time. The fundamental attribution error is the tendency to overestimate internal causes and underestimate external causes when judging others. Self-serving bias means crediting one’s success to internal factors and blaming failure on external factors.

**Marking points**

- Definition of attribution theory
- Distinctiveness
- Consensus
- Consistency
- Internal versus external causes
- Fundamental attribution error
- Self-serving bias

**Source:** MGT112 Chapter 10, attribution section

## Question 3: Model answer and marking guide

**Question:** Compare Maslow’s hierarchy, McGregor’s Theory X and Theory Y, Herzberg’s two-factor theory, and McClelland’s acquired-needs theory.

**Model answer**

Maslow proposed physiological, safety, social, esteem, and self-actualization needs and argued that a satisfied need loses motivating power. Theory X assumes employees dislike work and need control, while Theory Y assumes employees can enjoy work, accept responsibility, and use self-direction. Herzberg separated motivators such as achievement, recognition, and responsibility from hygiene factors such as policy, supervision, relationships, and working conditions. McClelland proposed acquired needs for achievement, power, and affiliation.

**Marking points**

- Maslow and five needs
- Theory X
- Theory Y
- Herzberg motivators
- Herzberg hygiene factors
- McClelland achievement, power, affiliation
- Clear comparison

**Source:** MGT112 Chapter 11, early theories

## Question 4: Model answer and marking guide

**Question:** Explain Fiedler’s contingency model and the factors used to evaluate the leadership situation.

**Model answer**

Fiedler’s model proposes that effective group performance depends on matching a leader’s task-oriented or relationship-oriented style with the situation. The least-preferred coworker questionnaire identifies style. Situational favorableness depends on leader-member relations, task structure, and position power. Task-oriented leaders perform best in very favorable or very unfavorable situations, while relationship-oriented leaders perform best in moderately favorable situations. Fiedler viewed leadership style as relatively fixed, so the situation may need to be changed.

**Marking points**

- Contingency idea
- LPC and style
- Leader-member relations
- Task structure
- Position power
- Task-oriented fit
- Relationship-oriented fit
- Style relatively fixed

**Source:** MGT112 Chapter 12, Fiedler section
