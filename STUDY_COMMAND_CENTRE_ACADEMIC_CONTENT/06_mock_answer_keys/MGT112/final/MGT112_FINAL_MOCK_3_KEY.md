## Question 2: Model answer and marking guide

**Question:** Explain the main perceptual shortcuts and the four methods managers use to shape behavior.

**Model answer**

Perceptual shortcuts include selective perception, which filters information through interests and experience; assumed similarity, which judges others through one’s own characteristics; stereotyping, which judges a person by perceived group membership; and the halo effect, which creates an overall impression from one characteristic. Managers shape behavior through positive reinforcement, which adds a desirable consequence; negative reinforcement, which removes an undesirable consequence; punishment, which applies an undesirable consequence; and extinction, which removes the reinforcement maintaining behavior.

**Marking points**

- Selective perception
- Assumed similarity
- Stereotyping
- Halo effect
- Positive reinforcement
- Negative reinforcement
- Punishment
- Extinction
- Correct distinctions

**Source:** MGT112 Chapter 10, perception and learning sections

## Question 3: Model answer and marking guide

**Question:** Explain equity theory and expectancy theory and show how each affects employee motivation.

**Model answer**

Equity theory says employees compare their outcomes-to-inputs ratio with a referent. Perceived inequity can change effort, output, quality, absenteeism, or turnover. Distributive justice concerns reward amounts, while procedural justice concerns the fairness of the allocation process. Expectancy theory says motivation depends on expectancy that effort will produce performance, instrumentality that performance will produce a reward, and valence that the reward is attractive. Motivation is weak if any link is weak.

**Marking points**

- Outcomes-to-inputs comparison
- Referent
- Responses to inequity
- Distributive justice
- Procedural justice
- Expectancy
- Instrumentality
- Valence
- All links required

**Source:** MGT112 Chapter 11, equity and expectancy sections

## Question 4: Model answer and marking guide

**Question:** Compare transactional, transformational, charismatic, and visionary leadership, then explain why trust is essential.

**Model answer**

Transactional leaders motivate through exchanges and rewards. Transformational leaders inspire extraordinary outcomes and encourage followers to question old assumptions. Charismatic leaders are enthusiastic and self-confident, articulate a vision, accept risk, and display unusual behavior. Visionary leaders create and communicate a realistic, credible, and attractive future. Trust is belief in a leader’s integrity, character, and ability. Its dimensions are integrity, competence, consistency, loyalty, and openness. Leaders build trust through honesty, fairness, consistency, keeping promises, confidentiality, and openness.

**Marking points**

- Transactional
- Transformational
- Charismatic
- Visionary
- Correct comparisons
- Definition of trust
- Five trust dimensions
- Methods of building trust

**Source:** MGT112 Chapter 12, modern leadership and trust
