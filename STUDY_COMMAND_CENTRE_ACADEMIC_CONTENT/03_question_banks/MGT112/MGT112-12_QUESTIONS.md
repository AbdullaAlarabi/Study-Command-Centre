## Essay bank
### MGT112-C12-ESSAY-01
- **Topic:** Behavioral leadership
- **Source slides:** 9-10
- **Question:** Compare the University of Iowa, Ohio State, University of Michigan, and Managerial Grid approaches.

**Model answer**

Iowa identified democratic, autocratic, and laissez-faire styles and initially favored democratic leadership. Ohio State identified consideration and initiating structure and found the high-high style often effective. Michigan distinguished employee-oriented and production-oriented leadership and linked employee orientation with higher productivity and satisfaction. The Managerial Grid measures concern for people and concern for production and identifies the 9,9 style as strongest.

**Marking points**
- Iowa styles
- Ohio State dimensions
- Michigan dimensions
- Managerial Grid
- Conclusions/comparison

### MGT112-C12-ESSAY-02
- **Topic:** Contingency theories
- **Source slides:** 13-19
- **Question:** Describe the four major contingency leadership theories.

**Model answer**

Fiedler matches a fixed task- or relationship-oriented style to situations defined by leader-member relations, task structure, and position power. Situational leadership matches telling, selling, participating, or delegating to follower ability and willingness. The leader-participation model uses decision rules and task contingencies to choose the amount of participation. Path-goal theory uses directive, supportive, participative, or achievement-oriented behavior to clarify goals and remove obstacles according to follower and environmental conditions.

**Marking points**
- Fiedler
- SLT
- Leader-participation
- Path-goal
- Contingency principle

### MGT112-C12-ESSAY-03
- **Topic:** Modern leadership
- **Source slides:** 22-23
- **Question:** Compare transactional, transformational, charismatic, and visionary leadership.

**Model answer**

Transactional leaders use exchanges, goals, and rewards. Transformational leaders inspire followers to exceed ordinary expectations and rethink established views. Charismatic leaders use confidence, enthusiasm, vision, risk taking, sensitivity, and unusual behavior to influence followers. Visionary leadership creates and articulates a realistic, credible, attractive future that mobilizes skills and resources.

**Marking points**
- Transactional
- Transformational
- Charismatic
- Visionary
- Clear distinctions

### MGT112-C12-ESSAY-04
- **Topic:** Trust
- **Source slides:** 29
- **Question:** Define trust, explain its five dimensions, and describe how leaders build it.

**Model answer**

Trust is belief in a leader’s integrity, character, and ability. Its dimensions are integrity, competence, consistency, loyalty, and openness. Leaders build trust by practicing openness, being fair, speaking honestly, telling the truth, acting consistently, keeping promises, protecting confidences, and demonstrating confidence and competence.

**Marking points**
- Trust definition
- Integrity
- Competence
- Consistency
- Loyalty
- Openness
- At least four trust-building actions
