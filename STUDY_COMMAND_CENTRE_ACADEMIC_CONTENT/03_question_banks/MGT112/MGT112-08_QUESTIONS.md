## Essay bank
### MGT112-C08-ESSAY-01
- **Topic:** HRM process
- **Source slides:** 5-9
- **Question:** Describe the key components of the human resource management process and the important influences on that process.

**Model answer**

The HRM process includes employment planning, recruitment, downsizing, selection, orientation, training and development, performance appraisal, and compensation and benefits. The early activities bring the right people into the organization, orientation and training develop them, and appraisal and compensation help sustain performance. The process is influenced by external factors, especially legal requirements, economic and technological conditions, and workforce changes.

**Marking points**
- Definition/purpose of HRM
- All eight activities
- Explanation of stages
- External/legal influences

### MGT112-C08-ESSAY-02
- **Topic:** Job analysis
- **Source slides:** 12
- **Question:** Explain job analysis and distinguish between a job description and a job specification.

**Model answer**

Job analysis examines workflows and identifies the skills and behaviors required for a job. Its results are used to prepare job descriptions and job specifications. A job description explains what the job holder does, how the work is done, and why it is done. A job specification lists the minimum knowledge, skills, attitudes, and qualifications needed to perform successfully.

**Marking points**
- Job analysis definition
- Purpose
- Job description
- Job specification
- Clear distinction

### MGT112-C08-ESSAY-03
- **Topic:** Selection
- **Source slides:** 18-19
- **Question:** Discuss the main selection devices and explain how interviews can be made more effective.

**Model answer**

Selection devices include written tests, performance-simulation tests, and interviews. Work sampling copies routine job tasks; assessment centers simulate managerial problems. Interviews should be structured and job-related, use the same prepared questions, begin with review of the job and résumé, require careful listening, and be evaluated immediately. Behavioral or situational interviews improve prediction by observing responses to job situations.

**Marking points**
- Written tests
- Performance simulations
- Interviews
- At least four interview improvements
- Behavioral interview

### MGT112-C08-ESSAY-04
- **Topic:** Retention
- **Source slides:** 20-28
- **Question:** Explain how organizations can retain competent, high-performing employees.

**Model answer**

Retention depends strongly on fair performance management and compensation. Performance standards and appraisal methods identify results and problems. Managers should match solutions to causes such as job mismatch, inadequate training, or discipline. Compensation should reflect skills, responsibility, performance, and fairness and may include skill-based pay, variable pay, and benefits. Supportive orientation, training, realistic expectations, inclusion, and work-life practices also reduce turnover.

**Marking points**
- Performance management
- Matching response to performance cause
- Fair compensation
- Benefits
- Training/orientation/work-life support
