### MGT112-C10-ESSAY-01
- **Topic:** OB
- **Source slides:** 5-6
- **Question:** Define organizational behavior and explain its focus, goals, and main employee outcomes.

**Model answer**

Organizational behavior is the study of the actions of people at work. It focuses on individual behavior, group behavior, and organizational systems. Its goals are to explain why behavior occurs, predict responses, and influence behavior. Important outcomes include productivity, absenteeism, turnover, organizational citizenship behavior, job satisfaction, and workplace misbehavior.

**Marking points**
- Definition
- Three focus areas
- Explain/predict/influence
- Six outcomes

### MGT112-C10-ESSAY-02
- **Topic:** Personality and EI
- **Source slides:** 13-14
- **Question:** Explain the Big Five personality model and emotional intelligence.

**Model answer**

The Big Five are extraversion, agreeableness, conscientiousness, emotional stability, and openness to experience. They describe stable personality differences and help predict work behavior, with conscientiousness especially linked to performance. Emotional intelligence is the ability to understand and manage emotions and includes self-awareness, self-management, self-motivation, empathy, and social skills.

### MGT112-C10-ESSAY-03
- **Topic:** Attribution
- **Source slides:** 21-22
- **Question:** Describe attribution theory and the main attribution errors.

**Model answer**

Attribution theory explains whether behavior is judged as internally or externally caused. Managers examine distinctiveness, whether the behavior is unusual for the person; consensus, whether others behave similarly; and consistency, whether it occurs repeatedly. The fundamental attribution error overemphasizes personal causes when judging others. Self-serving bias credits personal success to internal causes and blames failure on external causes.

**Marking points**
- Internal/external attribution
- Distinctiveness
- Consensus
- Consistency
- Fundamental error
- Self-serving bias

### MGT112-C10-ESSAY-04
- **Topic:** Learning
- **Source slides:** 26-29
- **Question:** Explain operant conditioning, social learning, and the four ways managers shape behavior.

**Model answer**

Operant conditioning says voluntary behavior is shaped by its consequences. Social learning occurs through observation and direct experience using attention, retention, motor reproduction, and reinforcement. Positive reinforcement adds something pleasant after desired behavior. Negative reinforcement removes something unpleasant after desired behavior. Punishment adds a penalty after undesirable behavior. Extinction withholds reinforcement so behavior fades.

**Marking points**
- Operant conditioning
- Social learning
- Four social-learning processes
- Positive reinforcement
- Negative reinforcement
- Punishment
- Extinction
