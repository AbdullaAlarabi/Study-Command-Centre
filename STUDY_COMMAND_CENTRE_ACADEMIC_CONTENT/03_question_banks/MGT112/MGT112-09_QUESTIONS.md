## Essay bank
### MGT112-C09-ESSAY-01
- **Topic:** Group development
- **Source slides:** 4-6
- **Question:** Define a group and describe the five stages of group development.

**Model answer**

A group is two or more interacting and interdependent individuals who come together to achieve goals. Forming involves joining and defining purpose and leadership. Storming contains conflict over control and direction. Norming develops close relationships, cohesion, and common expectations. Performing directs the established structure toward task completion. Adjourning occurs when temporary groups prepare to disband.

**Marking points**
- Group definition
- Forming
- Storming
- Norming
- Performing
- Adjourning

### MGT112-C09-ESSAY-02
- **Topic:** Formal groups
- **Source slides:** 4
- **Question:** Define a group and provide examples of different types of formal groups and their characteristics.

**Model answer**

Formal groups are defined by the organization and have assigned tasks. Command groups contain employees reporting to one manager. Task groups are often temporary and formed for a particular task. Cross-functional teams combine people from different specialties. Self-managed teams operate with substantial independence and perform managerial responsibilities such as scheduling and evaluating work.

**Marking points**
- Formal group definition
- Command
- Task
- Cross-functional
- Self-managed
- Characteristics/examples

### MGT112-C09-ESSAY-03
- **Topic:** Groups versus teams
- **Source slides:** 15
- **Question:** Compare work groups and work teams.

**Model answer**

A work group mainly shares information and makes decisions to help individual members perform. Its performance is the sum of individual contributions. A work team pursues a specific common goal through positive synergy, complementary skills, and individual and mutual accountability. Teams are more flexible and are especially useful when tasks need different skills, judgment, and joint effort.

**Marking points**
- Work group
- Work team
- Synergy
- Accountability
- Complementary skills
- When teams are useful

### MGT112-C09-ESSAY-04
- **Topic:** Effective teams
- **Source slides:** 17-21
- **Question:** Explain the main factors that make teams effective.

**Model answer**

Effective teams require a supportive context, suitable composition, motivating work design, and sound processes. Context includes resources, leadership and structure, trust, and rewards. Composition includes abilities, personality, roles, size, flexibility, and preferences. Work design includes autonomy, skill variety, whole tasks, and impact. Processes include common purpose, specific goals, efficacy, useful task conflict, and reduced social loafing.

**Marking points**
- Context
- Composition
- Work design
- Processes
- Examples within each
