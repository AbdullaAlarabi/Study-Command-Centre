## Essay bank
### MGT112-C11-ESSAY-01
- **Topic:** Motivation
- **Source slides:** 4
- **Question:** Define motivation and explain its three elements.

**Model answer**

Motivation is the process by which a person’s efforts are energized, directed, and sustained toward attaining a goal. Energy is the intensity or drive behind effort. Direction means the effort supports organizational goals. Persistence means the individual continues effort over time despite difficulty.

**Marking points**
- Definition
- Energy
- Direction
- Persistence
- Link to goals

### MGT112-C11-ESSAY-02
- **Topic:** Early theories
- **Source slides:** 6-10
- **Question:** Compare Maslow’s hierarchy, Theory X and Theory Y, Herzberg’s two-factor theory, and McClelland’s three-needs theory.

**Model answer**

Maslow proposes five levels from physiological to self-actualization and argues a satisfied need loses motivating power. McGregor’s Theory X assumes people dislike work and need control, while Theory Y assumes they can enjoy work, accept responsibility, and direct themselves. Herzberg separates intrinsic motivators that create satisfaction from hygiene factors that prevent dissatisfaction. McClelland identifies acquired needs for achievement, power, and affiliation.

**Marking points**
- Maslow
- Theory X
- Theory Y
- Herzberg motivators/hygiene
- McClelland three needs
- Comparison

### MGT112-C11-ESSAY-03
- **Topic:** JCM
- **Source slides:** 14-16
- **Question:** Explain the job characteristics model.

**Model answer**

The model identifies skill variety, task identity, task significance, autonomy, and feedback. The first three create meaningfulness. Autonomy creates personal responsibility, and feedback provides knowledge of results. When these psychological states exist, especially for employees with strong growth needs, internal motivation, performance, and satisfaction rise and absenteeism and turnover decline.

**Marking points**
- Five dimensions
- Meaningfulness
- Responsibility
- Knowledge of results
- Outcomes
- Growth need

### MGT112-C11-ESSAY-04
- **Topic:** Expectancy theory
- **Source slides:** 18-20
- **Question:** Explain expectancy theory and its three relationships.

**Model answer**

Expectancy theory says people choose actions based on expected outcomes and their attractiveness. Expectancy is the perceived probability that effort will produce performance. Instrumentality is the belief that performance will produce a reward. Valence is the value of that reward to the individual. Motivation is high only when the employee believes effort can succeed, performance will be rewarded, and the reward is personally desirable.

**Marking points**
- Theory definition
- Expectancy
- Instrumentality
- Valence
- Need for all three links
