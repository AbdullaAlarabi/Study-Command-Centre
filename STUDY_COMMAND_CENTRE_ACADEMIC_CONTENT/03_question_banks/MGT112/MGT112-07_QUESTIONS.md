## Essay bank
### MGT112-C07-ESSAY-01
- **Topic:** Six elements
- **Source slides:** 4-15
- **Question:** Describe the six key elements of organizational structure.

**Model answer**

The six elements are work specialization, departmentalization, authority and responsibility, span of control, centralization versus decentralization, and formalization. Work specialization divides activities into tasks. Departmentalization groups jobs. Authority gives the right to direct work and must be matched with responsibility. Span of control is the number of employees a manager can supervise effectively. Centralization or decentralization determines where decisions are made. Formalization is the degree of job standardization and rule guidance.

**Marking points**
- Definition of organizational structure/design
- Work specialization
- Departmentalization
- Authority and responsibility
- Span of control
- Centralization/decentralization
- Formalization

### MGT112-C07-ESSAY-02
- **Topic:** Power
- **Source slides:** 10-12
- **Question:** What is power, and what are the five types of power?

**Model answer**

Power is an individual’s capacity to influence decisions. Coercive power is based on fear or punishment. Reward power comes from the ability to provide rewards. Legitimate power comes from formal position. Expert power comes from knowledge and expertise. Referent power comes from identification with a respected or attractive person.

**Marking points**
- Correct definition of power
- Coercive
- Reward
- Legitimate
- Expert
- Referent
- Brief explanation of each

### MGT112-C07-ESSAY-03
- **Topic:** Design models
- **Source slides:** 17-21
- **Question:** Compare mechanistic and organic organizations.

**Model answer**

A mechanistic organization is rigid and tightly controlled, with high specialization, fixed duties, clear hierarchy, narrow spans, centralization, many rules, formal communication, and taller levels. An organic organization is adaptive and flexible, with collaboration, adaptable duties, few rules, informal communication, decentralization, wider spans, and a flatter structure. Mechanistic designs fit stability and cost control; organic designs fit innovation and uncertain environments.

**Marking points**
- Mechanistic definition
- Organic definition
- At least four correct contrasts
- Suitable environment/strategy

### MGT112-C07-ESSAY-04
- **Topic:** Traditional structures
- **Source slides:** 23-25
- **Question:** Compare the simple, functional, and divisional structures.

**Model answer**

A simple structure has low departmentalization, wide spans, centralized authority, and little formalization; it is fast and inexpensive but becomes overloaded as the firm grows. A functional structure groups similar specialties and gains economies of scale but can create silos. A divisional structure creates separate units with accountable managers and a focus on results but duplicates resources and increases cost.

**Marking points**
- Simple structure with strength/weakness
- Functional structure with strength/weakness
- Divisional structure with strength/weakness
- Clear comparison
