## Essay bank
### MKT112-C14-ESSAY-01
- **Topic:** Promotion mix
- **Source slides:** 3-8
- **Question:** Define the promotion mix and explain its five major tools.

**Model answer**

The promotion mix is the specific blend of communication tools used to engage consumers, communicate value, and build relationships. Advertising is paid nonpersonal promotion. Sales promotion uses short-term incentives. Personal selling uses direct sales-force interaction. Public relations builds favorable relations and image. Direct and digital marketing creates immediate, targeted, personalized, and interactive engagement.

**Marking points**
- Promotion mix definition
- Advertising
- Sales promotion
- Personal selling
- Public relations
- Direct/digital marketing

### MKT112-C14-ESSAY-02
- **Topic:** IMC/process
- **Source slides:** 9-16
- **Question:** Explain integrated marketing communications and the nine elements of the communication process.

**Model answer**

IMC carefully coordinates all channels and touch points to deliver a clear, consistent, and compelling company and brand message. The communication process includes sender, encoding, message, media, decoding, receiver, response, feedback, and noise. Communication works best when the sender understands the audience’s field of experience and uses feedback to correct or improve the message.

**Marking points**
- IMC definition/need
- Sender
- Encoding
- Message/media
- Decoding/receiver
- Response/feedback
- Noise

### MKT112-C14-ESSAY-03
- **Topic:** Communication development
- **Source slides:** 17-26
- **Question:** Describe the steps in developing effective marketing communications.

**Model answer**

The marketer identifies the target audience, determines communication objectives, designs message content and presentation, chooses personal or nonpersonal channels and media, selects a credible source, and collects feedback about awareness, recall, attitudes, buying, discussion, and visits. The target audience shapes what, how, when, where, and by whom the message is delivered.

**Marking points**
- Audience
- Objectives
- Message design
- Channels/media
- Source
- Feedback

### MKT112-C14-ESSAY-04
- **Topic:** Budget and mix
- **Source slides:** 27-33
- **Question:** Explain the four promotion-budget methods and compare push and pull strategies.

**Model answer**

The affordable method spends what the company believes it can afford. Percentage-of-sales uses a share of current or expected sales. Competitive parity matches competitors. Objective-and-task defines objectives, tasks, and costs. A push strategy promotes to channel members so they carry and promote the product. A pull strategy promotes directly to consumers so demand pulls the brand through retailers and wholesalers.

**Marking points**
- Four methods
- Push definition/tools
- Pull definition/tools
- Clear contrast
