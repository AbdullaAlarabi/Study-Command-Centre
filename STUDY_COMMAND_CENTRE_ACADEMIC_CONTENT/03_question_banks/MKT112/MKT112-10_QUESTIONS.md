## Essay bank
### MKT112-C10-ESSAY-01
- **Topic:** Price
- **Source slides:** 4-6
- **Question:** Define price and explain the main considerations in setting it.

**Model answer**

Price is the money charged or the value exchanged for the benefits of a product or service. It is the only revenue-producing marketing-mix element. Customer-perceived value sets the ceiling, product cost sets the floor, and the company chooses between them after considering competitors, strategy, objectives, marketing mix, market conditions, and demand.

**Marking points**
- Definition
- Revenue importance
- Value ceiling
- Cost floor
- Competition
- Strategy/market/demand

### MKT112-C10-ESSAY-02
- **Topic:** Pricing strategies
- **Source slides:** 6-18
- **Question:** Compare customer value-based, cost-based, and competition-based pricing.

**Model answer**

Value-based pricing begins with customer needs and perceived value, sets a target price, determines allowable costs, and designs the offer. Cost-based pricing begins with product cost and adds a return, using methods such as cost-plus or break-even pricing. Competition-based pricing sets prices after examining competitors’ strategies, costs, prices, offers, and relative customer value.

**Marking points**
- Value-based sequence
- Cost-based sequence
- Competition-based definition
- Strengths/limitations
- Clear comparison

### MKT112-C10-ESSAY-03
- **Topic:** Cost pricing
- **Source slides:** 13-17
- **Question:** Explain fixed, variable, and total costs, cost-plus pricing, and break-even pricing.

**Model answer**

Fixed costs do not change with output, while variable costs change directly with production. Total cost is their sum. Cost-plus pricing adds a standard markup to unit cost; it is simple and appears fair but can ignore demand and competitor prices. Break-even or target-return pricing uses expected revenue and costs to identify the sales volume needed to cover costs or earn a target return.

**Marking points**
- Fixed cost
- Variable cost
- Total cost
- Cost-plus
- Advantages/limitation
- Break-even/target return

### MKT112-C10-ESSAY-04
- **Topic:** Pricing factors
- **Source slides:** 19-27
- **Question:** Discuss the internal and external factors affecting pricing decisions.

**Model answer**

Internal factors include overall marketing strategy, objectives, positioning, product, distribution, promotion, target costing, and who has pricing authority. External factors include market structure, demand, price elasticity, competitors, economic conditions, inflation, interest rates, reseller needs, government regulation, and social concerns. All decisions should maintain customer value and fit the full marketing program.

**Marking points**
- Internal strategy/mix
- Target costing
- Organizational responsibility
- Market/demand
- Elasticity
- Economy
- Resellers/government/social concerns
