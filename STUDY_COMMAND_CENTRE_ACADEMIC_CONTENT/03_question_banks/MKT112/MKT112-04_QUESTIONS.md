## Essay bank
### MKT112-C04-ESSAY-01
- **Topic:** MIE
- **Source slides:** 5-10
- **Question:** Define the marketing information ecosystem and explain its main parts.

**Model answer**

A marketing information ecosystem is the people, processes, and assets used to assess information needs, develop needed information, and help managers apply it to actionable customer and market insights. It begins with information users, balances desired and necessary information, develops data through internal databases, competitive intelligence, and primary or secondary research, and supports analysis, decisions, and customer relationships.

**Marking points**
- Definition
- Information users/needs
- Internal databases
- Competitive intelligence
- Marketing research
- Analysis and use

### MKT112-C04-ESSAY-02
- **Topic:** Research process
- **Source slides:** 15-29
- **Question:** Describe the four steps in the marketing research process.

**Model answer**

First, define the problem and set exploratory, descriptive, or causal objectives. Second, develop a written research plan specifying information needs, data sources, approaches, contact methods, sampling, instruments, cost, and decision use. Third, implement the plan by collecting, processing, and analyzing data. Fourth, interpret and report useful findings jointly with managers.

**Marking points**
- Define problem/objectives
- Develop plan
- Implement/collect/analyze
- Interpret/report
- Importance of correct problem

### MKT112-C04-ESSAY-03
- **Topic:** Data and approaches
- **Source slides:** 19-24
- **Question:** Compare primary and secondary data and discuss the main primary research approaches.

**Model answer**

Secondary data already exists and is fast and inexpensive but may be irrelevant, inaccurate, outdated, or biased. Primary data is collected for the specific problem. Observation studies actual behavior, including ethnography and netnography. Surveys ask questions and are suited to descriptive information. Experiments use matched groups and treatments to test causal relationships.

**Marking points**
- Secondary definition
- Secondary pros/cons
- Primary definition
- Observation
- Survey
- Experiment

### MKT112-C04-ESSAY-04
- **Topic:** Information sources
- **Source slides:** 11-13
- **Question:** Explain internal data, competitive marketing intelligence, and marketing research.

**Model answer**

Internal data comes from company records such as sales, customer characteristics, website visits, service, cost, inventory, and point-of-sale information. Competitive marketing intelligence continually scans publicly available consumer, competitor, and environmental information for opportunities, threats, and competitor actions. Marketing research is a focused, systematic study designed for a specific marketing decision.

**Marking points**
- Internal data with examples
- Advantages/limitations
- Competitive intelligence purpose
- Ethics
- Marketing research definition/distinction
