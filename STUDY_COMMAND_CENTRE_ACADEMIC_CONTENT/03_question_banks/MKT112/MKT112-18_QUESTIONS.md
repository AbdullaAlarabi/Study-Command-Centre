## Essay bank
### MKT112-C18-ESSAY-01
- **Topic:** Competitor analysis
- **Source slides:** 5-10
- **Question:** Describe the steps in competitor analysis.

**Model answer**

Competitor analysis has three main steps: identify competitors using both product and broader market views; assess their objectives, strategies, strategic groups, strengths, weaknesses, and likely reactions; and select which competitors to attack or avoid. Customer value analysis can compare important attributes, and intelligence must be collected and updated continuously.

**Marking points**
- Identify competitors
- Assess objectives/strategies
- Strengths/weaknesses
- Reaction patterns
- Select attack/avoid
- Customer value analysis

### MKT112-C18-ESSAY-02
- **Topic:** Competitor intelligence
- **Source slides:** 7-11
- **Question:** Explain how a company assesses competitors and designs a competitive intelligence system.

**Model answer**

Assessment uses secondary data, experience, word of mouth, customer and dealer research, websites, social media, and benchmarking. The company evaluates objectives, strategies, performance, strengths, weaknesses, philosophy, and probable reactions. An intelligence system identifies needed information and sources, collects it continually, checks validity and reliability, interprets and organizes it, distributes it to decision makers, and answers inquiries.

**Marking points**
- Information sources
- Objectives/strategies
- Strengths/weaknesses
- Reactions
- Benchmarking
- System functions

### MKT112-C18-ESSAY-03
- **Topic:** Competitive positions
- **Source slides:** 22-30
- **Question:** Compare market leader, challenger, follower, and nicher strategies.

**Model answer**

Leaders expand total demand, protect share, and expand share profitably. Challengers attack leaders or smaller firms directly or indirectly and may improve on pioneer ideas. Followers avoid costly battles, copy or improve successful programs, control costs, and add distinctive value. Nichers specialize in profitable segments by customer, market, product, service, quality, price, or mix.

**Marking points**
- Leader
- Challenger
- Follower
- Nicher
- Examples of tactics
- Clear comparison

### MKT112-C18-ESSAY-04
- **Topic:** Orientations
- **Source slides:** 31-36
- **Question:** Compare competitor-centered, customer-centered, and market-centered companies.

**Model answer**

A competitor-centered company tracks rival moves and can be a strong fighter but becomes reactive. A customer-centered company studies customer needs and identifies long-term opportunities and relationships. A market-centered company balances customer and competitor information and seeks innovative ways to create superior value. This balanced market orientation is generally the strongest.

**Marking points**
- Competitor-centered strength/weakness
- Customer-centered
- Market-centered
- Reason balanced orientation is preferred
