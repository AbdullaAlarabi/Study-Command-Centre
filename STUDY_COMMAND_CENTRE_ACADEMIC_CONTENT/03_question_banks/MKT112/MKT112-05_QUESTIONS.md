## Essay bank
### MKT112-C05-ESSAY-01
- **Topic:** Buyer model
- **Source slides:** 4-5
- **Question:** Explain the model of consumer buyer behavior.

**Model answer**

Marketing stimuli—product, price, place, and promotion—and other economic, technological, social, and cultural stimuli enter the buyer’s black box. The black box contains buyer characteristics and the buyer decision process. These produce responses such as attitudes and preferences, what, when, where, and how much the buyer purchases, and later brand engagement and relationships.

**Marking points**
- Marketing stimuli
- Other stimuli
- Black box characteristics
- Decision process
- Buyer responses

### MKT112-C05-ESSAY-02
- **Topic:** Influence factors
- **Source slides:** 7-18
- **Question:** Discuss the four major factors influencing consumer behavior.

**Model answer**

Cultural factors include culture and subculture. Social factors include groups, social networks, opinion leaders, family, roles, and status. Personal factors include age, life stage, occupation, economic situation, lifestyle, personality, and self-concept. Psychological factors include motivation, perception, learning, beliefs, and attitudes. Marketers cannot control most of these factors but must understand them.

**Marking points**
- Cultural
- Social
- Personal
- Psychological
- Examples/marketing relevance

### MKT112-C05-ESSAY-03
- **Topic:** Buyer process
- **Source slides:** 23-29
- **Question:** Describe the five stages of the buyer decision process.

**Model answer**

Need recognition occurs when a problem or need is triggered. Information search uses personal, commercial, public, and experiential sources. Evaluation of alternatives compares brands. Purchase decision selects a brand but can be affected by other people or unexpected events. Postpurchase behavior depends on expectations versus perceived performance and may create satisfaction, dissatisfaction, or cognitive dissonance.

**Marking points**
- Need recognition
- Information search
- Evaluation
- Purchase
- Postpurchase
- Cognitive dissonance

### MKT112-C05-ESSAY-04
- **Topic:** New products
- **Source slides:** 32-37
- **Question:** Explain the adoption process, adopter categories, and characteristics affecting the rate of adoption.

**Model answer**

The adoption stages are awareness, interest, evaluation, trial, and adoption. Adopter groups are innovators, early adopters, early mainstream, late mainstream, and lagging adopters. Adoption is faster when the innovation has clear relative advantage, fits consumer values and experience, is not overly complex, can be tried on a limited basis, and has visible or communicable results.

**Marking points**
- Five adoption stages
- Five adopter groups
- Relative advantage
- Compatibility
- Complexity
- Trialability
- Observability
