# Chapter 14: Integrated Marketing Communications

## Rapid review
The promotion mix is the specific blend of communication tools used to engage consumers, communicate customer value, and build relationships.

### Five promotion tools
1. Advertising
2. Sales promotion
3. Personal selling
4. Public relations
5. Direct and digital marketing

### Integrated marketing communications (IMC)
IMC coordinates communication channels to provide a clear, consistent, and compelling message. It is needed because audiences are fragmented, consumers use many information sources, and digital channels create many touch points.

### Nine communication elements
Sender, encoding, message, media, decoding, receiver, response, feedback, noise.

### Steps in developing effective communication
1. Identify target audience.
2. Determine objectives.
3. Design message.
4. Choose channels and media.
5. Select message source.
6. Collect feedback.

Objectives may move the buyer through awareness, knowledge, liking, preference, conviction, and purchase.

### Channels and budgets
- Personal channels include direct contact, experts, advocates, opinion leaders, friends, and family.
- Nonpersonal channels include media, atmospheres, and events.
- Budget methods: affordable, percentage-of-sales, competitive parity, objective-and-task.

### Push and pull
- Push directs selling and trade promotion toward channel members.
- Pull directs advertising and consumer promotion toward final consumers.

Communication should be truthful, responsible, and free from deception, bribery, false claims, bait-and-switch, or unfair attacks.

## Must remember
- Five tools
- Meaning of IMC
- Nine elements
- Six communication-development steps
- Four budget methods
- Push versus pull

# Chapter 17: Digital Marketing

## Rapid review
Digital marketing uses the internet, mobile networks/devices, and social media to engage targeted consumers, communities, and businesses.

### Four-part digital process
1. Understand digital marketing.
2. Prepare a digital campaign.
3. Use digital channels in an omni-channel strategy.
4. Address public-policy issues.

### Benefits
For buyers: convenience, privacy, information, choice, immediacy, interaction, and community. For sellers: reach, speed, flexibility, personalization, relationships, low cost, and real-time response.

### Preparing a campaign
- Build digital consumer personas.
- Understand omni-channel navigation across the customer journey.
- Use experimentation and A/B testing.

### Digital channels
- Online marketing: websites, online ads, search ads, email, online video, blogs.
- Social media: targeted, interactive, fast, shareable, but user controlled and capable of backfiring.
- Mobile marketing: reaches consumers anywhere and anytime.

### Omni-channel marketing
Creates a seamless experience across store, online, mobile, and other channels. It requires aligned structures, incentives, business model, and marketing mix.

### Public-policy issues
Irritation, unfairness, deception, fraud, privacy, data integrity, and security. Trust requires transparency and choice.

## Must remember
- Digital definition and four-part process
- Buyer and seller benefits
- Persona
- Omni-channel navigation
- A/B testing
- Online, social, mobile
- Omni-channel requirements
- Policy issues

# Chapter 18: Creating Competitive Advantage

## Rapid review
Competitive advantage comes from delivering more value and satisfaction than competitors. Competitor analysis is the process of identifying, assessing, and selecting key competitors.

### Identifying competitors
Competitors may make the same product, satisfy the same need, or compete for the same consumer spending. A market view is broader than a product view.

### Assessing competitors
Study:
- Objectives
- Strategies
- Strengths and weaknesses
- Reaction patterns

A strategic group contains firms following a similar strategy. Benchmarking compares products and processes with competitors or leading firms. Customer value analysis compares performance on attributes customers value.

### Competitive intelligence system
Identify sources, collect information, check validity/reliability, interpret and organize it, distribute it to decision makers, and answer inquiries.

### Basic strategies
- Overall cost leadership
- Differentiation
- Focus

Value disciplines:
- Operational excellence
- Customer intimacy
- Product leadership

### Market positions
- Market leader
- Market challenger
- Market follower
- Market nicher

Leaders expand demand and protect or grow share. Challengers attack leaders or smaller firms. Followers adapt successful strategies with lower cost. Nichers specialize in profitable segments unattractive to large firms.

### Orientations
- Competitor-centered: follows rivals closely but may become reactive.
- Customer-centered: focuses on customer needs and long-term opportunities.
- Market-centered: balances customer and competitor understanding.

## Must remember
- Three competitor-analysis steps
- What to assess
- Strategic group and benchmarking
- Customer value analysis
- Competitive intelligence steps
- Cost leadership, differentiation, focus
- Three value disciplines
- Leader, challenger, follower, nicher
- Three orientations
