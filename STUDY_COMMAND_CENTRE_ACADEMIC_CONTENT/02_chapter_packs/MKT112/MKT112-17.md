# MKT112 Chapter 17: Digital Marketing

## Chapter in one minute
This chapter presents the digital marketing process, digital consumer research, online, social, mobile, and omni-channel marketing, experimentation, consumer personas, and the privacy, deception, fraud, and security issues created by digital marketing.

## 1. Digital marketing process and definition
- Digital marketing uses technology-intensive platforms such as the internet, mobile networks and devices, and social media to engage targeted consumers, communities, and businesses.
- The process includes understanding digital marketing, preparing a campaign, using digital channels in an omni-channel strategy, and addressing public-policy issues.
- Digital marketing can be a complete business model or can complement physical operations.

## 2. Benefits
- For buyers: convenience, privacy, broad information and product access, interactivity, immediacy, price competition, and community.
- For sellers: low-cost and fast reach, flexibility, personalization, relationship building, access to difficult markets, and real-time marketing linked to moments and trends.

## 3. Preparing a campaign
- Digital consumer personas are detailed representations of prototypical customers used to guide campaign ideas and decisions.
- Each target segment should have its own persona including needs, behavior, online habits, influences, goals, concerns, and preferred brands.
- Consumer omni-channel navigation behavior is the use of multiple digital and nondigital channels across the buying process.
- Marketers must identify the channels used at each stage and track the journey as well as possible.

## 4. Experimentation
- Digital settings allow rapid A/B testing by exposing comparable groups to different treatments and measuring results.
- Experiments should minimize noise and interactions between treatment and control groups.

## 5. Online marketing
- Online marketing uses websites, online ads and promotions, email, online video, and blogs.
- Marketing websites move consumers toward purchase or another outcome. Branded community websites create engagement and community around the brand.
- Online advertising includes display and search-related ads.
- Email can deliver targeted and personalized relationship messages; spam is unwanted commercial email.
- Online video can support viral word of mouth, but marketers cannot guarantee that content will go viral.
- Blogs are online journals focused on defined topics.

## 6. Social and mobile marketing
- Social media are online networks where people share messages, opinions, images, video, and other content.
- Advantages include targeting, personal contact, interactivity, speed, cost efficiency, engagement, and sharing.
- Social media is user controlled. Brands must earn participation through valuable content, and campaigns can backfire.
- Mobile marketing delivers messages and content through phones, tablets, and other devices, engaging customers anywhere and anytime.

## 7. Omni-channel strategy
- Omni-channel marketing creates a seamless cross-channel experience integrating in-store, online, and mobile shopping.
- It requires alignment with the business model, organizational structures and incentives, and the entire marketing mix.
- Channel teams should support total customer experience rather than compete against one another.

## 8. Public-policy issues
- Irritation includes spam, clutter, pop-ups, and unwanted content.
- Unfairness includes taking advantage of impulsive or less-sophisticated buyers.
- Deception uses misleading content; fraud includes identity theft and financial scams.
- Privacy concerns arise when marketers collect enough data to exploit or manipulate consumers.
- Transparency, choice, data integrity, and security are required to maintain trust.
